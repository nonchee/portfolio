Intro to Nancy: A Web Tutorial

Getting Started

Nancy is one of many friendly Computer Science students at the University of Pennsylvania.

She took her first programming class thinking she'd come out knowing how to build games to help her little brother, but apparently Computer Science can help out with neuroimaging and data analysis as well. How exciting!

Nancy's best work comes when she gets to combine what she knows about programming, design, and cognition together to make something super cool.

Setting Up

Nancy is easy to obtain on your computer! Ask her to design something, program something, or edit your paper, and she will jump at the chance to try something new.

If neither of those are handy, all you have to do is entertain her with a horrendous pun or an interesting-looking fruit and she will pretty much follow you wherever.

Meet Pennjamin, the PennApps Penguin. I made Pennjamin during a thirty-day free trial of Adobe Illustrator, and everyone on the PennApps design team got so carried away branding everything with penguins that he ended up with his own Facebook page.

Pennjamin probably went through four or five iterations of various roundness, eye size, and beak style. Developing him was a lot fun as I paid attention to critique from older designers on the team. Towards the end though, they mostly just started going "it's so cuuuuuute".
